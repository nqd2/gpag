# 10-Seasonality

There are multiple times during a year when one could notice seasonal patterns. The January Effect. Stocks often rise in January as investors repurchase stocks after selling them in December for tax harvesting. The End of Quarter Effect.

Funds may rebalance their portfolios during the last week of a quarter, possibly creating predictable price patterns. There is a pre-holiday effect or the Santa Claus rally. Stocks have been known to rise around the year-end holidays. Then there is sell in May and go away until Halloween.

This trend suggests investors may sell stocks on May 1st and return at October's end. Seasonality can also occur around events like earnings announcements.
