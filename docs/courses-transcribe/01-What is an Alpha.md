# 1-What is an Alpha

An alpha is a mathematical model that seeks to predict the future price movement of various financial instruments. An alpha is a vector of predicted values of stocks in the universe. The universe could be a group of US stocks defined on the basis of their liquidity. Each value can change daily and has two properties.

First, the direction of the alpha and second, the magnitude of the alpha. Capitalization determines whether you want to go long or short the instrument. The magnitude of the values determine what proportion of the allocated capital should be distributed amongst the instruments in the universe. To create an alpha, we begin with an idea that seeks to capture an inefficiency in the market.

That idea is then applied to a chosen group of stocks which we call our alpha universe.
